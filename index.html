Why This Fits Rural Banking

A trust layer for receipts, shared screenshots, and document proofs

01

Stops fake payment screenshots

A forged "paid" screen can mislead merchants or BC agents. Your existing watermark plus signature pipeline turns the visual proof itself into a bank-verifiable asset.

02

Works across low-trust sharing channels

In rural workflows, proof often moves through WhatsApp, gallery shares, screenshots, or printed PDFs. The perceptual fingerprinting path is designed for exactly that kind of instability.

03

Keeps verification simple

The user does not need to understand cryptography. They only need a verifier that says whether the payment proof, banking slip, or statement is authentic or tampered.

Project Flow

The same Digital Trust Shield flow, now anchored to a payment-success journey

Step 1

Payment gateway confirms success

The flow starts when a rural banking app, UPI gateway, or CSP system displays Payment Successful. That receipt image or PDF becomes the content we need to protect before sharing.

Step 2

generate_keys.py creates the bank authority identity

An RSA key pair is generated. The private key stays sealed with Windows DPAPI, while the public key can be distributed to verifier apps used by bank staff, field agents, or merchants.

Step 3

utils.py computes a stable visual fingerprint

Instead of signing raw bytes, the system canonicalizes the receipt or statement visual, removes watermark carrier noise, and builds a dHash + pHash style fingerprint that survives moderate recompression.

Step 4

sign_poster.py signs the banking proof

The bank signs the SHA-256 digest of that fingerprint using RSA-PSS. This becomes the cryptographic proof that the displayed receipt or document was issued by the trusted authority.

Step 5

watermark_embedder.py hides proof inside the visual

The signed payload is embedded invisibly across DCT blocks in the luminance channel, so the trust proof stays inside the receipt screenshot, passbook image, or transaction confirmation itself.

Step 6

Sharing happens through real rural channels

Customers, merchants, and BC agents may forward the proof through WhatsApp, save it as JPEG, export it as PDF, or resend screenshots. The design expects those transformations.

Step 7

watermark_extractor.py and verify_poster.py validate it later

The verifier extracts the hidden signature, recomputes the current fingerprint, checks RSA validity with the public key, and compares distances to decide whether the banking proof is authentic or fake.

Step 8

pdf_support.py handles multi-page banking documents

Statements, sanction letters, claim forms, or subsidy PDFs can be signed page by page, then verified page by page, so a tampered document reveals the exact compromised page.

Step 9

Audit logs preserve accountability

Signing and verification events are written to hash-chained logs, helping the bank prove who signed what, when it was verified, and whether someone tried to alter trust records.

Same Codebase, New Context

How each existing file maps into the rural banking solution

generate_keys.py

Creates the bank or payment-authority RSA key pair that anchors trust.

sign_poster.py

Signs payment receipts, QR success screens, and exported banking visuals before they are shared.

verify_poster.py

Verifies whether a received receipt or proof-of-payment image is authentic or tampered.

watermark_embedder.py

Hides the signed trust bundle invisibly inside the banking image using DCT-based embedding.

watermark_extractor.py

Recovers the hidden trust proof after screenshotting, compression, resizing, or forwarding.

pdf_support.py

Extends the same trust flow to multi-page statements, certificates, and rural banking PDFs.

utils.py

Holds the key sealing, perceptual fingerprinting, signing helpers, verification thresholds, and audit logging backbone.

Decision States

The frontend can clearly explain what the verifier concludes

Authentic

Signed by the trusted authority

Signature validates, the extracted fingerprint matches the received receipt closely, and the proof survived sharing.

Forwarded But Valid

Compressed, resized, still trusted

The receipt changed format during WhatsApp or gallery sharing, but the watermark extractor and fingerprint thresholds still confirm authenticity.

Fake or Tampered

Edited content or invalid signature

If someone changes amount, beneficiary, page content, or inserts a false screenshot, the signature or fingerprint comparison will fail.

Honest Limitation

Strong prototype today, extensible platform tomorrow

This frontend presents the project as an adaptive rural banking cybersecurity framework, but the current core is still a prototype engine. The next production step would be exposing these Python modules through a secure backend API, linking them to payment events, and distributing a lightweight verifier app for field use.
